The European Medicines Agency's (EMA) Guideline on good pharmacovigilance practices (GVP), Module IV requires that risk-based audits of the quality system be performed at regular intervals to assure that it complies with the established quality requirements and to determine its effectiveness. It includes audit of the pharmacovigilance system which is covered by the quality system. The GVP Modules are applicable to EU-based companies and any company marketing medicinal products on a global basis. The legally required risk based audit strategy shall cover all PV processes and tasks undertaken by or delegated to other departments, MAH affiliates, and third parties such as distributors, external service providers, partners (the PV Universe). The PV Audit Strategy Plan is used to prepare the PV audit program, i.e. annual PV Audit Schedule.

In this two day workshop conference we will review the EMA and FDA requirements regarding Risk Based Audits of the PV system and Quality System. Tim Engler on NomicChain: a blockchain based on Martin-Löf type theory


Date: June 21, 2018

Description:

Tim Engler will give an informal talk about NomicChain, a framework for a decentralized, self-modifying blockchain based on Martin-Löf type theory that encodes the rules for how to process the chain within the chain itself.

From Tim:

Imagine a set of expressions that act as a "constitution" or "bill of rights". Each expression would commonly be a function that takes, along with common arguments, one or more proofs of propositions and returns a command that describes an alteration to the "constitution" itself (i.e., data describing a creation of a new expression, or an alteration/deletion of an existing one).

Now, place this structure in a decentralized p2p network in the format of a blockchain, where each block contains calls to the expression set, thus providing instructions to alter itself.

In Idris, this could be represented by a private data structure (which can't be created directly):

data CoreCommand : Type where
AlterCommand : {type : Type} -> (namePath : NamePath) -> (value : type) -> CoreCommand
DeleteCommand : (namePath : NamePath)

where NamePath represents a canonical way to identify a named global expression and is defined as something like:

NamePath : Type
NamePath = List String

This talk would discuss some ideas about:

* A detailed description on how NomicChain could be built.
* Potential uses for NomicChain. For example, how it could unify the fragmented market of cryptocurrencies, and creating a common service to provide security and communication.
* The differences between so-called "Proof of Work", and "Proof of Stake", and why I think NomicChain should be initially be designed to work a neutral basis between the two.
* Why I think that NomicChain should ultimately be built without a coin
(this ties into the fragmentation of the cryptocurrency market).

After this talk, I'm hoping for feedback as far as the implementation details, as well as the potential uses for a system such as this. I know the some of basics of type theory, but am an amateur at best, and would like to have opinions from others who are more familar with it.

How to Build a Chatbot and Deploy It to Slack


Date: July 9, 2018

Description:

In this joint hands-on IBM-Slack workshop, we will build a chatbot powered by artificial intelligence and IBM Watson services. You will learn about the building blocks needed to understand natural language user input and how to process requests via a Slackbot. We will cover how Watson Assistant and Watson Tone Analyzer services enable this capability, and deploy the chatbot to Slack. This is a fun, hands-on session you don't want to miss!

Speaker Bio:

Marek Sadowski is a full stack developer advocate, a robotics startup founder and an entrepreneur. He has about 20 year experience in consulting largest enterprises in USA, Europe, Japan, Middle East and Africa. As a graduate from the International Space University Marek pioneered in a research on VR goggles for the virtual reality system to control robots on Mars in NASA Ames. He founded a startup to deliver robotics solutions and services for industries. Marek moved to Silicon Valley to promote IoT and robotics solutions driven by AI and Cloud.
